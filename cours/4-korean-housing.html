Conclusion

Korean housing has undergone significant transformation from traditional structures to modern residential complexes. This evolution reflects broader social, economic, and cultural changes in Korean society. Current challenges in the housing market, unique rental systems, and ongoing redevelopment efforts continue to shape the future of Korean residential architecture and urban planning.

The interaction between traditional values, modern needs, and economic factors creates a complex housing environment that requires careful consideration of both preservation and development needs while addressing current market challenges and social requirements.
